Workshop Recap: Remote Work Tips for Gen Z

13 Sep 2025
Table of Contents
    • Key Takeaways
    • Action Framework
    • Freelance Header Example
    • Personal Notes & Insights

Today, I attended a quick workshop on remote work for Gen Z at Kedai Dee Aksara from 3:00 PM to 5:00 PM WIB, led by Kak Naufal Fakhri (LinkedIn).

He shared his experience of working remotely for the past 2 years and why remote work can be a game-changer:

  • Time flexibility: Work when you’re most productive.
  • Global opportunities: Join digital companies worldwide.
  • Flexibility: Balance work and life while staying productive.

Key Takeaways

  1. Find your niche first
    Identify what you’re best at and focus your portfolio around it. A strong portfolio is more important than a CV in the digital freelancing world — think of it as CV 2.0.

  2. Platforms to find remote work

    Project-based: Upwork, Fiverr, Fastwork
    Full-time remote: Glints, Toptal, We Work Remotely, Remote.com
    Startups: Tech in Asia
    Direct applications: Indeed, LinkedIn, or company websites

  3. Skills & essentials

    • English fluency is mandatory — practice continuously.
    • Portfolio: Show real evidence of your work.

Action Framework

Kak Naufal recommended a simple 3-step approach to kickstart your freelance career:

  1. Prepare – Build 3 types of portfolio samples.
  2. Learn – Invest in bootcamps or short courses to strengthen skills.
  3. Problem → Proof → CTA – Present a challenge you solved, show the proof in your portfolio, and include a clear Call-to-Action.

Freelance Header Example

Use this format for a 1-line freelance headline:

[Role] who helps [target audience] achieve [specific outcome] in [timeframe]

Examples inspired by the workshop:

  • Network Engineer who helps small-to-medium businesses validate and deploy resilient, low-downtime network architectures under one week.
  • Network Trainer who helps 30-student cohorts achieve an 80% MTCNA pass rate in a 2-day intensive bootcamp.

Personal Notes & Insights

  • Work style: Introverts might prefer investing in a cozy workspace, while extroverts (like Kak Naufal) thrive in cafes and social settings.
  • Portfolio proof is key — even dummy projects can demonstrate skills.
  • Framing & branding: Be result-oriented and quantify your achievements where possible.
  • Specialization matters: Choose what you excel at and refine it continuously.

Remote work is not just about flexibility — it’s about strategic preparation, continuous learning, and measurable results. Following this approach, Gen Z freelancers can confidently navigate the global remote work landscape.
